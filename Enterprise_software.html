Customer Relationship Management

 CRM systems are used to offer insights to businesses on their customers in order to improve their relationships with their customers, thus helping them to streamline their business and improve their profitability. Online CRM has vastly improved the insights that are available to businesses. It is also more cost effective and accessible from all devices, which is of great benefit to salespeople, for example. Examples of CRM systems include Salesforce and Microsoft Dynamics 365. They allow business to get a good overview of their customers through analysing data from sales, customer service, marketing and social media. CRM allows businesses to get all the information for a single customer from different interactions across the board, whether it is through their purchase preferences, customer service or their social media interactions. It is a more holistic approach to customer interaction and allows a business to more accurately forecast and plan for the future. 

Payments systems

Successful online businesses will offer as many payment options as possible in order to catch as many customers as they can.
Internet merchant accounts-Businesses can offer credit card payments to their customers if they set up through local banks. They may need to go through a number of banks since some banks only deal with certain credit cards.
Third Party Merchant Accounts-Businesses can get merchant accounts set up by a third party, such as Beanstream or PSiGate. There are a number of advantages to doing this: they don’t require security deposits, can be set up quickly and can come with an internet gateway for credit card processing and a shopping cart. A big disadvantage is that they come with a lot of fees.
PayPal-is an all in one payment option which can be suitable for small businesses who want to set up online trading quickly and easily. They accept Visa, MasterCard or American Express and they also offer a PayFlow Gateway and express checkout.
Stripe-is another example of a checkout platform. It differs from PayPal in that the customer is not directed to another site. The business keeps them on their website to complete their sale.
Checkout by Amazon-When businesses sell through Amazon, their customers are redirected to Checkout by Amazon to complete their transaction. The more sales a business has, the lower the transaction rate is for the business.
It is important for retailers to also offer payment options for customers who do not wish to pay online such as over the phone payments or postal payments.

Supply Chain management (SCM) systems

Supply chain management involves the flow of goods throughout a manufacturing or services process from raw materials right through to the final product for the customer.
A traditional SCM system is linear with 5 stages- Planning, sourcing, manufacturing, delivery and returning.  The ideal for any company is to have a just-in-time supply chain. Products need to be produced to meet customer demands without exceeding it. This reduces waste, cost and time to the manufacturer.
Modern SCM is network based rather than linear. It is customer based, supplying the goods that the customer wants and when they want it. An important component of modern SCM is connectedness with the customer through social media sites, feedback, reviews and by offering multiple buying options, whether in shops, online or postal. Another component is collaboration with suppliers using cloud based network systems. This allows the manufacturer to find supplies of raw materials at the right quantity and the right price.
An effective SCM system therefore, involves the management of data. This affects the product quality, cost delivery, customer satisfaction and ultimately, profitability. It also helps a company to forecast trends e.g. seasonal trends and events such as Black Friday and Cyber Monday. It can help a company prepare for unpredicted or unprecedented events such as Brexit, the Covid pandemic or the current Ukrainian conflict. The future successes of a business will ultimately depend on An SCM system which can accurately forecast events and the ability to adopt to the future needs of their customers.
