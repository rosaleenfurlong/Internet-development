History of the Internet

October 4, 1957

Soviet Union launches Sputnik

February, 1958

Advance Research Projects Agency (ARPA) created to regain technological advantage over USSR.

1963

ASCII first developed – converts binary to human language.

1965

Packet switching developed in M.I.T. – data is broken down into packets before it is sent to its destination.

October 29, 1969

ARP net sends first message from UCLA to Stanford.

1969

4 computers connected to ARPAnet.

1971

University of Hawaii’s ALOHAnet added.

1972

Ray Tomlinson invents email.

1973

London’s University College and Royal Radar Establishment in Norway added to network. (First non-American link)

1973

Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) developed by Vinton Cerf. Later Internet Protocol (IP) added. Allows all computers on network to identify and communicate with each other.

1976

Ethernet developed.

1977

First PC modem introduced.

1984

Domain Name System (DNS) allows easy to remember-domain-names be converted to IP addresses.

1989

AOL is launched.

1990

First commercial dial-up Internet provider, World is launched and ARPAnet ceases to exist.

1991

Tim Berners-Lee develops the World Wide Web, a system of sharing information and is the internet as we know it today.
MP3 is developed.

1992

Students at University of Illinois developed Mosaic browser – words and pictures on same page, scrollbars and clickable links.

1992

Internet can be used for commercial purposes. Companies begin to set up websites, e-commerce commences.

1994

Yahoo! created. First blog created.

1995

eBay and Amazon launched. The Telegraph publishes the first online newspaper. Craig’s List founded.

1996

Hotmail introduced. First mobile phones with internet created.

1997

Wi-Fi invented.

1998

Google and Napster launched.

2001

Wikipedia is launched.

2002

LinkedIn launched. Creative Commons first used. Creators can keep their copyright while sharing their work more freely.

2003

Skype launched allowing VOIP calling. Myspace launched.

2004

Web 2.0 begins, characterised by more dynamic webpages and the growth of social media. Facebook launched.

2005

YouTube launched – free video hosting.

2006

Spotify and Twitter launched.

2007

Hulu launched – makes popular shows available to watch online. IPhone launched, renewing interest in mobile internet. Gmail developed.

2008

Chrome released by Microsoft windows.

2010

China dominates internet usage with 457 million users by the end of the year, rising to 989 million in 2020. Pinterest and Instagram launched.

2010-2011

Arab Spring – Anti-government protests that began in Tunisia and spread rapidly to other countries across north Africa and the Middle East due to social media.

2011

Siri, the first modern AI voice assistant was launched,

2013

Edward Snowden leaked information from the national Security Agency in the US regarding a mass surveillance program.

2014

Amazon’s Echo launched.

2018

Tik Tok launched.

2019

The internet is 50.

 

 

2020’s

Web 3.0 – decentralization of data, creating a more transparent environment, and giving ownership of data to individuals.

Cryptocurrencies-internet currencies not based on any centralised government, low transfer fees. Examples are Bitcoin and Ethereum.

Blockchain – Shared ledger where transactions are recorded between members. Transactions are clear, secure and transparent, each transaction is stored as a block of data and blocks are linked together in a “chain”.

Venmo, Revolut cash transfers.

Teams, Zoom increasingly used for online business and education, particularly since Covid pandemic.

5g mobile – more secure, stable internet.
